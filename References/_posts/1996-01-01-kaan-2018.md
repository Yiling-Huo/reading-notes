@book{kaan2018syntactic,
    abstract = {Listening to other people talk, we very quickly get used to their accent, word choice, or specific meaning of phrases. It is less clear to what extent listeners and readers adapt to abstract syntactic structures: do sentences with a particular structure get easier to process as more sentences of the same type have been seen or heard? Recent models of sentence processing propose that syntactic adaptation, language change, and language learning are driven by the same mechanisms. It is therefore no surprise that syntactic adaptation has drawn a lot of recent interest in the field of language processing research. In this chapter we review and discuss the evidence put forward for syntactic adaptation, mainly in comprehension. We then discuss the relation between syntactic adaptation as studied in the laboratory, and language adaptation and learning in daily life. Finally, we discuss and list some factors that may underlie various forms of adaptation, and sketch directions for future research.},
